## Abstract

The competitive ground of digital visibility has shifted from webpages to AI-generated answers and increasingly toward autonomous actions performed by AI agents.

This paper proposes a unified framework integrating AEO, GEO, and Agentic Optimization (AgO) under a single theoretical perspective based on delegated consumer–AI agency.

The framework introduces Brand Erasure as a strategic risk in AI-mediated markets and develops propositions linking semantic visibility, information gain, interoperability, and attribution persistence to brand outcomes inside AI systems.

The article also proposes managerial implications, new KPI structures, and a research agenda focused on AI visibility auditing, semantic equity, and agentic discoverability.

## Research propositions

### Proposition 1. Epistemic Effort

Holding semantic relevance and entity authority constant, an AI agent’s probability of citing or selecting a source decreases as the verification cost of its claims increases.

### Proposition 2. Information Gain

Sources whose informational contribution cannot be cheaply substituted from the rest of the retrieved corpus achieve higher share-of-citation in synthesized responses.

### Proposition 3. Definitional Anchoring

Brands that introduce or operationalize terminology that becomes load-bearing in synthesized answers receive citations at disproportionate rates.

### Proposition 4. Interoperability Premium

Platforms exposing richer machine-readable affordances achieve higher agent execution rates.

### Proposition 5. Erasure Asymmetry

Brands with stronger entity-graph presence experience lower erasure rates in synthesized responses and agentic completions.

### Proposition 6. Hybrid Gatekeeping

Brands combining semantic anchoring with selective gatekeeping achieve higher long-term attribution persistence.
